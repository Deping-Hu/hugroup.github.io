Deping Hu

Principal Investigator

depinghu@bnu.edu.cn
Google Scholar

Deping received his B.Sc. degree in polymer materials and engineering from Anhui University, China in 2012. Then, he went to the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences and got master (2015) and PhD (2018) degrees in theoretical chemistry under the supervision of Prof. Zhenggang Lan. During that time, he studied the theoretical description of photo-induced ultrafast nonadiabatic dynamics within the framework of mix-quantum-classical dynamics approaches and direct dynamics. He also made some initial efforts to combine the machine learning technology and nonadiabatic dynamics simulation to treat realistic polyatomic molecules.

After he got his Ph.D. degree, he joined the group of Prof. Zhigang Shuai as a postdoc at Tsinghua University. His researches were still relevant to photoinduced reactions of molecular systems, while he started to understand the excited-state process of organic light-emitting materials. In 2020, he left Shuai’s group and joined Zhenggang Lan’s group as a research assistant at South China Normal University. There, he worked on the on-the-fly non-adiabatic dynamics with the semi-classical method based on the mapping Hamiltonian, he also tried to simulate the nonlinear spectroscopy using the on-the-fly dynamics. He joined the group of Pengfei Huo as a postdoc at University of Rochester in 2021, his researches focused on the development of non-adiabatic mix-quantum-classical dynamics methods for cavity quantum electrodynamics.

Since 2023, he joined Beijing Normal University. His group aims at the development and applications of non-adiabatic dynamics methods to study the photophysical and photochemical processes of molecular and material systems.
